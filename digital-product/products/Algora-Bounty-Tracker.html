📅 Filtering Rules (Decision Tree)

Use this before claiming ANY bounty. Takes 90 seconds.

1. Is the bounty ≥ $100?
   └── NO  → Skip unless extraordinary circumstances
   └── YES → Continue

2. Is it in your tech stack OR can AI close the gap in <2hrs?
   └── NO  → Skip unless 5x budget multiplier
   └── YES → Continue

3. Is the description specific enough to scope without asking questions?
   └── NO  → Skip. Ambiguous = scope creep.
   └── YES → Continue

4. Has the repo had a commit/comment in the last 30 days?
   └── NO  → Risky. Unresponsive maintainer = stuck bounty.
   └── YES → ✅ Worth claiming.

🚨 Red Flags — Skip These Immediately

  • No dollar amount attached
  • Issue older than 60 days with zero maintainer response
  • “Just improve performance” (unquantifiable goal)
  • Requires signing a CLA (legal overhead delays payment)
  • “Good first issue” in a dead repo

💡 Tips From Real Bounty Hunting

Claim fast, submit slow (but not too slow). The /attempt lock lasts 72 hours on most bounties. Don’t waste the lock by submitting a half-baked PR. But also don’t take 2 weeks — someone else will open a competing PR.

One clean PR > three messy PRs. Maintainers remember names. A clean first PR gets your second claim approved faster.

Read the repo’s contributing.md before touching code. Nothing wastes time like submitting a PR that violates the repo’s format rules.

Track your $/hr in real-time. If a bounty is taking longer than expected, calculate your current effective rate. Decide if you should continue or cut it.

Don’t chase big numbers blindly. $1,000 bounties that take 3 weeks might pay less per hour than three $200 bounties that each take 4 hours.
